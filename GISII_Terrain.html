Terrain Analysis

This was one of my favourite assignments in my GIS II class. Topography maps have always been my favorite to look at so playing around with elevation data is always fun. The assignment was to analyze Duluth's terrain to rate the level of danger for roads in winter and to plot the best route to the UMD campus depending on if you are walking or biking.

The map to the left is a simple contour map that looks nice and detailed from the hillshade effect on it.

Depending on if you are biking or walking in Duluth the route you take is important due to the differing slopes of roads. On a bike one would probably take a gradual sloped route even it was longer and if walking I thought it would be easier to take the short and steep route. For this assignment it was specifically looking for the best route from Burrito Union (local restaurant) to the UMD Campus. These two routes are definitely the fastest, but if I was actually at Burrito Union I would walk along Chester Creek through the park to get to UMD because it is a very nice walk. The creek can be seen along the left side of the aerial photo.

pic of chester here

Roads in Duluth can be treacherous, the ice/snow on the steep roads can make your car go backwards in some cases. It was determined that road sections that had a slope greater than 15% and an aspect angle between 270 and 90 degrees were the most dangerous. All colored sections fit those parameters and as you can see on the map there is a lot of sections that dangerous.
