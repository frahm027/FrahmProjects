Geostatistical Analysis

I believe this assignment was my first encounter with the Geostatiscal Analyst tool in ArcMap. Since then I have definitely grown to appreciate how useful it can be and love the maps that it can produce. Based on points scattered over California with data on ozone pollution I was able to predict the levels of ozone pollution through out the whole state.

As you can see based on the map on the right, it is not a perfect system. The areas where there are very low levels of error are where I had point data, and it only gets less accurate the farther away from the point you look. The are ways to reduce the level of error but this assignment was simply an introduction to the tool, but I do get to use a lot more of it in my GIS Statistics class.
